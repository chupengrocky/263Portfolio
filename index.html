UX CASE STUDY: ReSell


"You can have the greatest technical system in the world. But if it hasn't taken into account the humanity of the user, it's not going to be a successful system."

--Matt Bietz



THE PROJECT

THE PROBLEM

Our team set out to investigate how local students could buy and sell used items in a safer, more trusted environment than is currently offered by other applications on the market.

OUR SOLUTION

An application prototype we call ReSell.

ReSell is a community-focused trading mobile app specifically for use by local students.

The goal of the design was to allow for easy communications and transactions between student users while maintaining a high level of trust.

DESIGN METHODS

Our team used a variety of methods to create the design of our ReSell application prototype.

First, we performed Market Research, investigating our design's competitors, interviewing potential users, and creating user personas.

We then created an interactive Wireframe Prototype that could be tested.

Lastly, we conducted User and Heuristic Evaluations to help refine our Prototype into a High-Fidelity Mockup.

MARKET RESEARCH

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

We researched potential competitors in the market space of trading applications to help us understand potential pain points that our application could address for our target users.

The top potential competitors we identified were Facebook Marketplace, Offerup, Craigslist, and Ebay.

Facebook Marketplace and Offerup offer a variety of services and safeguards for users but target a broad, generic range of users.

Meanwhile, Ebay and especially Craigslist offer a less restrictive and potentially anonymous environment for users that trades fewer safeguards for ease of use

With this analysis in mind, we decided our target users should be local students, believing that this would assist us in establishing trust between users of the app.

USER RESEARCH

We began interviewing potential users of our system to understand their thoughts regarding trading apps. After these interviews, we analyzed the data collected and found that some of the most common themes were a lack of trust, issues getting or sending their items, and lack of communication between buyer and seller.

USER PERSONAS

This user research we conducted then allowed us to produce five personas that summarize our user feedback, and analyzes the different components that may affect our future design.
